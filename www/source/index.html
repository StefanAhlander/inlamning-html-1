First a little about me

I was born in 1973 in Uppsala, Sweden, which makes me older than young.

I still remember the Commodore VIC 20 my father got me when I was a kid. I also remember writing my first programs in BASIC. When the Commodore 64 was introduced I pleaded with my parents to get one. A neighbouring boy a few years older than I had gotten into assembler programing and it seemed like magic to me. I was hooked and was writng code almost all my spare time. I was coding demos, hacking BBS's and cracking copy protecion on games.

Me teens and adolecent years were a bit messy but I finaly settled into law school. After graduating with a degree in law I worked different jobs, and lived in Thailand for 18 months. Lately I have worked for a law firm specialized in criminal law and currently for a one man firm specializing in migration law. I do a lot of pro bono work helping refugees.

In 2013 I was diagnosed with chronic renal failure. The illness has been progressing slowly but it's made me think about my choices in life and about what I want to do when I grow up. My mind allways goes back to my childhood passion for programing. Of course nothing will be like a childhood passion but I can't help but think that I should have made som different choices. Because I believe it's never too late I have decided to make an effort to change my career.
