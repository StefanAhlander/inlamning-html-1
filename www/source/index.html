First a little about me

I was born in 1973 in Uppsala, Sweden. That makes me older than young.

I had an early interest for computers and programing and still remember the Commodore VIC 20 my father got me. I also remember writing my first programs in BASIC. When the Commodore 64 was introduced I pleaded with my parents to get one. A neighbouring boy a few years older than me had gotten into assembler programing and it seemed like magic to me. I was hooked and was writng code almost all my spare time. I was coding demos, hacking BBS's and cracking copy protecion on games. Not everything was legal but computer security was not really a thing and the risk was low.

When I got older I realised that girls at that time wasn't very much into computer geeks. I therefor got into martial arts instead and soon forgott about programing.

Me teens and adolecent years were a bit messy but I finaly settled into law school. After graduating with a degree in law I worked different jobs and lived in Thailand for 18 months. Lately I worked for a law firm specialized in criminal law. I worked there for a year after which I switched to a small one man firm owned by a friend of mine.

In 2013 I was diagnosed with chronic renal failure. The illness has been progressing slowly but it's made me think about my choices in life and about what I want to do when I grow up. The thoughts allways goes back to my childhood passion for programing. Of course nothing will be like a childhood passion but I can't help that I should have made som different choices. Because I believe it's never too late I have decided to make an effort to change my career.
